Why This Matters

Githurai produces tons of waste daily. Most ends up in illegal dumpsites, blocked drainage, or burned‚Äîpolluting our air and water.

But what if that "waste" was actually opportunity?

The Economic Reality

üí∞ The Numbers:

  • 1 kg of plastic bottles = KES 30-40
  • One dedicated youth collector can earn KES 500-1,000 daily
  • A recycling crew of 5 can collect 50-100 kg per day
  • That's real income + a cleaner community

We're proving that environmental sustainability and economic opportunity aren't opposites‚Äîthey're partners.

Our Youth-Powered Model

We employ young people (including ex-offenders from our reintegration program) as:

  • Collection crew members - Daily wages for gathering recyclables
  • Sorting specialists - Paid per kg for categorizing materials
  • Community educators - Teaching neighbors about recycling benefits
  • Micro-entrepreneurs - Running their own collection routes

How It Works: From Street to Sale

Step 1: Collection

Our crews move through Githurai neighborhoods daily, collecting plastics, metals, and other recyclables from households and businesses.

Step 2: Sorting

Materials are brought to our sorting center where they're categorized by type and quality. This is where youth learn the business side‚Äîwhat's valuable, what buyers want, quality standards.

Step 3: Sale

We connect with recycling buyers across Nairobi, getting fair prices and building long-term partnerships.

Step 4: Payment

Crew members and individual collectors get paid based on quantity and quality. Transparent. Fair. Immediate.

The Climate Connection

Climate change isn't just about melting ice caps‚Äîit's about our community's daily reality:

  • Blocked drainage from plastic waste = flooding during rains
  • Burning plastic = toxic air we breathe
  • Illegal dumpsites = contaminated water sources
  • Urban heat = lack of trees and green spaces

Our recycling and tree-planting work addresses these issues while creating income. Climate justice means economic justice.

Challenges We Face

We're honest about obstacles:

  • Market fluctuations - Recycling prices vary, affecting income
  • Limited equipment - We need more collection bags, scales, transport
  • Weather dependency - Heavy rains disrupt collection
  • Competition - Other collectors target the same areas
  • Stigma - Some still see waste collection as "dirty work"

But every challenge is an opportunity to innovate and improve.
