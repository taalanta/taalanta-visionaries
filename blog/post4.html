The Science Behind Our Approach

Research shows that effective substance abuse prevention requires:

  • Purpose: Meaningful activities to replace drug use
  • Community: Social connection and belonging
  • Skills: Building competence and confidence
  • Income: Meeting basic needs reduces escapism
  • Hope: Belief in a better future

Our programs address all five.

Why Traditional Programs Fail

Most drug prevention programs in our context fail because they:

  • Focus on fear ("drugs will kill you") instead of hope
  • Offer nothing to replace the high‚Äîno alternative activities
  • Ignore economic realities (unemployment, poverty)
  • Stigmatize users, making them hide instead of seek help
  • Treat it as individual weakness, not systemic issue

We flip the script entirely.
